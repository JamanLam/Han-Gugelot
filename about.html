About
Hans Gugelot, a pioneering German industrial designer, was born in 1920 and is widely celebrated for his innovative and functional approach to design.

Throughout his career, Gugelot sought to create products that were both aesthetically pleasing and user-friendly, an approach which earned him a prominent position within the Bauhaus-inspired Ulm School of Design. His collaborations with companies like Braun and Dieter Rams resulted in iconic products such as the SK4 record player, affectionately known as the "Snow White's Coffin."

Gugelot's design philosophy, which championed simplicity, modularity, and user-centered design, had a profound impact on the world of industrial design, leaving a lasting legacy that continues to influence designers across the globe.
